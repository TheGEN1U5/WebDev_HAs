  • Games (By Genre)

    1. Sci-Fi
      Driver: San Francisco
      This game sets on a sci-fi plot of the player being able to shift between cars. The plot is that the crime lord Jericho is on the loose and where the player plays as a cop and not a normal one but the one who is in the realm of dreams and can do nothing but drive cars, as he can shift between cars being driven in the real world and can take control of different drivers around the city to gather clues to cathch the crime lord Jericho.

      Mortimer Becket's Time Paradox
      This is a game that was fun to play personally for me. The game is set on the sci-fi plot of the player's Grandfather inventing a Time Portal but accidentally leaving it open to cause ripples and disturbances in time, like peices of gadgets from the future ending up in the hands of Egyptian mummies. This is more or less an object finding game but the objects aren't in one peice and are scattered around different places in different times. The objective of the game is obviously to find these and return safely back home.
    2. Racing
      Burnout: Paradise City
      This franchise of the Burnout Series features the same objective, Drive, Race, Destruct BUT with awesome graphics and a bit of a plot related to DJ Atomica. You get to choose from 80 different cars (and 10 different motorbikes if you buy the full game) obviously that need to be earned. There are 6 differeent types of races and all of the races start using the special Burnout Shortcut i.e UpArrow + DownArrow. This is a superb game to pay and I recommend it to everyone.

      Need For Speed: Most Wanted (2007)
      The best part of NFS Franchise is the plot and yet again though very old Gameloft surprises us with a plot in which you are in a Need For Speed to get away from the cops save your Unique, Marvellous Car. Did you ask reason why the cops are behind you? It's simple. You were found illegally street racing.
    3. Creative/Adventure
      Jungle Boy
      This not so popular but fun to play game is a side scroller and is good for passing your time when you are held by the chains of ennui.

      Planet Coaster
      This is a game that allows you to build your dream Theme park and have fun managing clients, staffs and of course pickpockets. This game has a really interactive GUI and supports media being added to your park from external sources like your hard drive.

      Ready Player One
      It takes place in 2045, when much of humanity uses the virtual reality software OASIS (Ontologically Anthropocentric Sensory Immersive Simulation) to escape the desolation of the real world. Orphaned teenager Wade Watts (Sheridan) finds clues to a hidden game that promises OASIS's ownership to the winner, and he and four allies try to complete it before a corporation run by businessman Nolan Sorrento (Mendelsohn) can do so.

      X-Men is an American superhero film series based on the fictional superhero team of the same name, who originally appeared in a series of comic books created by Stan Lee and Jack Kirby and published by Marvel Comics. 20th Century Fox obtained the film rights to the characters in 1994, and after numerous drafts, Bryan Singer was hired to direct the first film, released in 2000, and its sequel, X2 (2003), while Brett Ratner directed X-Men: The Last Stand (2006).

      Jumanji (Franchise)
      Jumanji is an American media franchise, based on the children's books Jumanji (1981) and its sequel Zathura (2002), written by American Chris Van Allsburg. The franchise is owned by Columbia Pictures as a subsidiary of Sony Pictures. This plot of the franchise follows the adventures of various people who find themselves imperiled when playing Jumanji, that will either unleash its dangerous elements into the real world or trap the players within the game.
